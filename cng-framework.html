ENERGY TRANSITION • 2024

Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) Adoption Framework

National Petroleum Authority (NPA) – Gas Directorate • 2024

Project Overview

As Ghana works toward diversifying its energy mix and transitioning to cleaner fuels, Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) presents a promising opportunity. This project contributed to the early-stage development of regulatory, technical, and infrastructure frameworks needed for the safe and sustainable introduction of CNG in the downstream petroleum sector.

Objectives

  • Support the development of a clear regulatory and safety framework for CNG operations
  • Research international CNG programs and extract relevant lessons for the Ghanaian context
  • Identify key infrastructure, technical, and regulatory gaps that must be addressed
  • Contribute to institutional capacity building and stakeholder awareness on CNG

Approach & Implementation

The project combined technical research with practical regulatory work. I conducted extensive research on international CNG regulatory models, safety standards, and infrastructure development approaches from countries with established natural gas markets. Technical guidelines and safety requirements relevant to CNG handling, storage, and distribution were reviewed and adapted. The work also involved mapping potential infrastructure requirements and participating in internal discussions on how CNG could fit into Ghana’s existing energy landscape. Phased implementation pathways and stakeholder considerations were also explored.

Key Results

  • Contributed to foundational policy and regulatory thinking for CNG adoption in Ghana
  • Strengthened institutional knowledge on CNG infrastructure and safety requirements
  • Helped position the Authority to engage more confidently with potential investors and technical partners
  • Supported Ghana’s long-term goals of energy diversification and transition to cleaner fuels
  • Created internal reference materials that can guide future CNG-related policy development
