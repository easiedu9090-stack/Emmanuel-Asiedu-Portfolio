REGULATORY SYSTEMS • 2024

LPG Cylinder Recirculation Model (CRM) Monitoring Platform

National Petroleum Authority (NPA) • Accra, Ghana • 2024

Project Overview

Ghana introduced the Cylinder Recirculation Model (CRM) policy to improve safety, accountability, and efficiency in the LPG sector. However, effective implementation required stronger monitoring systems. This project focused on building digital tools to help the National Petroleum Authority track compliance across hundreds of LPG facilities across the country.

Objectives

  • Strengthen regulatory oversight of LPG facilities under the new CRM policy
  • Improve the consistency and efficiency of inspection data collection and reporting
  • Enable faster identification of non-compliant facilities for targeted enforcement
  • Support data-driven decision-making and continuous improvement of the CRM policy

Approach & Implementation

The project involved designing practical digital tools that could be used by field inspectors and regulatory officers. Excel-based monitoring databases were developed along with standardized inspection checklists to ensure consistent data capture across regions. A centralized compliance tracking system was introduced to monitor facility performance over time. Field inspections were conducted alongside regulatory teams to validate the tools and understand real operational challenges on the ground. The systems were designed to be simple for field use while still generating useful insights for management and policy refinement.

Key Results

  • Improved efficiency and consistency in tracking compliance across hundreds of LPG facilities nationwide
  • Enabled faster generation of compliance reports and better prioritization of enforcement activities
  • Contributed to a safer and more organized LPG distribution system under the Cylinder Recirculation Model
  • Strengthened the Authority’s internal capacity for ongoing monitoring, evaluation, and policy improvement
  • Created practical tools that supported both field operations and management-level decision-making
