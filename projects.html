DATA & ANALYTICS • 2025

Maintenance Data Management & Analytics System

Tontrac Plant and Engineering LTD • Accra, Ghana

Project Overview

Maintenance operations in heavy industries often suffer from fragmented records, inconsistent data, and limited visibility into equipment performance. This project addressed those challenges by designing and implementing a centralized maintenance data management and analytics system. The goal was to move from reactive, paper-based processes to a structured, data-driven approach that supports better planning, accountability, and long-term reliability improvement.

Objectives

  • Consolidate scattered maintenance records, lubrication schedules, work orders, and downtime data into a single, reliable database
  • Develop clear and actionable dashboards that give supervisors and management real-time visibility of maintenance performance
  • Reduce reliance on manual reporting and improve the accuracy and consistency of maintenance data
  • Lay the foundation for future predictive maintenance capabilities by creating clean, structured historical data

Approach & Implementation

The project began with a detailed assessment of existing maintenance workflows and data gaps. A normalized MySQL database was designed to capture equipment master data, maintenance history, lubrication activities, spare parts consumption, and failure events. Power BI was used to build interactive dashboards that tracked key metrics such as equipment availability, maintenance compliance rates, backlog trends, and recurring failure patterns. Standardized data entry templates and validation rules were introduced to improve data quality at the source. The system was implemented in phases, starting with critical equipment categories, and field teams were trained on proper data capture and system usage.

Key Results & Impact

  • Created a single source of truth for maintenance data, significantly reducing time spent searching for historical records
  • Enabled maintenance planners to identify problematic equipment and recurring issues more quickly through visual dashboards
  • Improved maintenance compliance tracking and accountability across teams
  • Reduced manual reporting effort and improved the reliability of performance reports presented to management
  • Established a solid data foundation that can support future predictive maintenance and condition monitoring initiatives
REGULATORY SYSTEMS • 2024

LPG Cylinder Recirculation Model (CRM) Monitoring Platform

National Petroleum Authority (NPA) • Accra, Ghana

Project Overview

Ghana’s Cylinder Recirculation Model (CRM) policy was introduced to improve safety, accountability, and efficiency in the LPG sector. However, effective implementation required stronger monitoring and data systems. This project focused on building digital tools and processes to help the National Petroleum Authority track compliance across hundreds of LPG facilities and support evidence-based regulatory decisions.

Objectives

  • Strengthen the Authority’s ability to monitor compliance with the Cylinder Recirculation Model across the country
  • Improve the consistency, speed, and quality of inspection data collection and reporting
  • Enable better identification of non-compliant facilities and support targeted regulatory interventions
  • Build internal capacity for data-driven regulation and continuous improvement of the CRM policy

Approach & Implementation

The project involved designing practical digital tools to support field inspectors and regulatory officers. Excel-based monitoring databases were developed and standardized inspection checklists were created to ensure consistent data capture. A centralized tracking system was introduced to monitor facility compliance status over time. Field visits were conducted alongside inspection teams to understand real operational challenges and validate the data being collected. The tools were designed to be simple enough for field use while still generating useful management-level insights.

Key Results & Impact

  • Improved the efficiency and consistency of compliance monitoring across a large number of LPG facilities
  • Enabled faster generation of compliance reports and better prioritization of enforcement activities
  • Contributed to a safer and more organized LPG distribution system under the new CRM policy
  • Strengthened the Authority’s internal systems for ongoing monitoring, evaluation, and policy refinement
  • Helped build institutional knowledge that supports long-term regulatory improvement
ENERGY TRANSITION • 2024

Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) Adoption Framework

National Petroleum Authority (NPA) – Gas Directorate

Project Overview

As part of Ghana’s broader energy transition strategy, this project contributed to the early-stage development of regulatory and technical frameworks required for the safe and sustainable introduction of Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) in the downstream sector. The work focused on building institutional readiness and identifying key requirements for future CNG deployment.

Objectives

  • Support the development of a clear regulatory and safety framework for CNG operations
  • Research international experiences and extract relevant lessons for the Ghanaian context
  • Identify infrastructure, technical, and regulatory gaps that must be addressed before large-scale CNG adoption
  • Contribute to internal capacity building and stakeholder awareness on CNG as a cleaner energy option

Approach & Implementation

The project combined technical research with practical regulatory work. Extensive review of international CNG programs was conducted, focusing on safety standards, infrastructure models, and regulatory approaches from countries with established natural gas markets. Technical guidelines and safety requirements relevant to CNG handling and storage were reviewed and adapted. The work also involved mapping potential infrastructure needs and participating in internal discussions on how CNG could fit into Ghana’s existing energy landscape. Stakeholder considerations and phased implementation pathways were also explored.

Key Results & Impact

  • Contributed to foundational policy and regulatory thinking around CNG adoption in Ghana
  • Strengthened the Authority’s technical understanding of CNG infrastructure and safety requirements
  • Helped position the institution to engage more confidently with potential investors and technical partners
  • Supported Ghana’s long-term goals of energy diversification and transition toward cleaner fuels
  • Created internal reference materials that can guide future CNG-related policy development
SIMULATION & CONTROL • 2022

Optimization of Bi-Directional Oil and Gas Drilling Equipment Using MATLAB

Final Year Project • All Nations University, Koforidua

Project Overview

This project focused on improving the accuracy and stability of bi-directional drilling equipment through simulation-based control system design. Downhole vibrations often cause drill bit positioning errors, which can lead to poor wellbore quality and increased operational costs. The work explored how advanced control techniques could be applied using MATLAB/Simulink to mitigate these effects without requiring expensive physical prototypes or field tests.

Aim

To develop a MATLAB/Simulink-based simulation model capable of controlling drilling string equipment and improving drill bit positioning accuracy under vibration disturbances.

Objectives

  • Design and implement a realistic drill string and bit model in Simulink using mass-spring-damper principles
  • Develop sensor signal processing modules to filter noise from downhole vibration data
  • Design and tune a Linear Quadratic Regulator (LQR) controller for stable and accurate drill bit positioning
  • Evaluate controller performance through simulation under realistic disturbance conditions

Methodology

The project followed a structured simulation-driven approach. A dynamic model of the drill string and bit was built in Simulink, incorporating torsional and axial vibration effects. Signal processing techniques were applied to simulate realistic sensor noise. An LQR controller was designed using state-space methods and tuned through iterative simulation. Root Locus analysis was used to understand system behavior, while a high-torque DC motor model was integrated to represent actuation. Multiple test scenarios were run to assess how effectively the controller could reject disturbances and maintain positioning accuracy.

Key Results Achieved

  • The LQR controller successfully reduced drill bit oscillation from approximately ±15° to near 0° within 35 seconds of simulation time.
  • The system demonstrated strong disturbance rejection and maintained stability under modeled downhole vibration conditions.
  • Simulation results confirmed that the designed controller could achieve fast response and good positioning accuracy without excessive control effort.
  • The project demonstrated that MATLAB/Simulink offers a practical, low-cost platform for testing and validating drilling control strategies before field implementation.
