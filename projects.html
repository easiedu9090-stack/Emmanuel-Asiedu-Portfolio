DATA & ANALYTICS • 2025

Maintenance Data Management & Analytics System

Tontrac Plant and Engineering LTD • Accra, Ghana

Overview

Maintenance operations in heavy industries often suffer from fragmented records and limited visibility into equipment performance. This project addressed those challenges by designing and implementing a centralized maintenance data management and analytics system to support better planning, accountability, and long-term reliability improvement.

Objectives

  • Consolidate scattered maintenance records, lubrication schedules, and work history into a single structured database
  • Develop interactive dashboards that provide real-time visibility of equipment performance and maintenance compliance
  • Reduce reliance on manual reporting and improve data accuracy
  • Create a solid data foundation for future predictive maintenance initiatives

Approach & Implementation

A normalized MySQL database was designed to capture equipment master data, maintenance work orders, lubrication activities, and downtime events. Interactive Power BI dashboards were developed to visualize key performance indicators such as equipment availability, maintenance compliance, and recurring failure patterns. Standardized data entry templates and validation rules were introduced to improve data quality. The system was implemented in phases with training provided to field teams and supervisors.

Key Results

  • Created a single source of truth for maintenance data across operations
  • Enabled faster identification of problematic equipment and recurring issues through visual dashboards
  • Improved maintenance compliance tracking and team accountability
  • Significantly reduced time spent on manual report generation
  • Established a strong foundation for future predictive maintenance capabilities
REGULATORY SYSTEMS • 2024

LPG Cylinder Recirculation Model (CRM) Monitoring Platform

National Petroleum Authority (NPA) • Accra, Ghana

Overview

Ghana’s Cylinder Recirculation Model (CRM) policy was introduced to improve safety and accountability in the LPG sector. This project focused on building digital tools and systems to help the National Petroleum Authority effectively monitor compliance across hundreds of LPG facilities nationwide.

Objectives

  • Strengthen regulatory oversight and monitoring of LPG facilities under the CRM policy
  • Improve the consistency and efficiency of inspection data collection and reporting
  • Support timely identification of non-compliant facilities for targeted enforcement
  • Enhance data-driven decision-making in LPG sector regulation

Approach & Implementation

Excel-based monitoring databases and standardized inspection checklists were developed to ensure consistent data capture. A centralized compliance tracking system was introduced to monitor facility performance over time. Field inspections were conducted alongside regulatory teams to validate data and understand operational realities. The tools were designed to be practical for field use while still generating useful management-level insights.

Key Results

  • Improved efficiency and consistency in tracking compliance across hundreds of LPG facilities
  • Enabled faster generation of compliance reports and better prioritization of enforcement activities
  • Contributed to safer and more organized LPG distribution under the CRM policy
  • Strengthened the Authority’s internal systems for ongoing monitoring and policy refinement
ENERGY TRANSITION • 2024

Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) Adoption Framework

National Petroleum Authority (NPA) – Gas Directorate

Overview

As part of Ghana’s energy transition efforts, this project contributed to the early development of regulatory and technical frameworks required for the safe introduction of Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) in the downstream sector. The focus was on building institutional readiness and identifying key requirements for future CNG deployment.

Objectives

  • Support the development of a clear regulatory and safety framework for CNG operations
  • Research international CNG programs and extract relevant lessons for Ghana
  • Identify infrastructure, technical, and regulatory gaps for successful CNG adoption
  • Contribute to internal capacity building and stakeholder awareness on CNG

Approach & Implementation

Extensive research was conducted on international CNG regulatory models, safety standards, and infrastructure approaches. Technical guidelines and safety requirements relevant to CNG handling and storage were reviewed. The work also involved mapping potential infrastructure needs and participating in internal discussions on how CNG could fit into Ghana’s energy landscape.

Key Results

  • Contributed to foundational policy and regulatory thinking for CNG adoption in Ghana
  • Strengthened institutional knowledge on CNG infrastructure and safety requirements
  • Helped position the Authority to engage more confidently with potential investors
  • Supported Ghana’s long-term goals of energy diversification and cleaner fuel transition
SIMULATION & CONTROL • 2022

Optimization of Bi-Directional Oil and Gas Drilling Equipment Using MATLAB

Final Year Project • All Nations University, Koforidua

Overview

This project developed a MATLAB/Simulink simulation framework to improve the positioning accuracy of bi-directional drilling equipment by actively controlling downhole vibrations and shocks using a Linear Quadratic Regulator (LQR) controller. The work explored how advanced control techniques could be tested through simulation as a low-cost alternative to expensive field testing.

Aim

To develop a MATLAB/Simulink model capable of controlling drilling string equipment and improving drill bit positioning accuracy under vibration disturbances.

Objectives

  • Design and implement a realistic drill string and bit model in Simulink
  • Develop sensor signal processing modules to filter downhole vibration noise
  • Design and tune an LQR controller for stable and accurate drill bit positioning
  • Evaluate controller performance through simulation under realistic disturbance conditions

Methodology

A dynamic model of the drill string and bit was built in Simulink using mass-spring-damper principles. Realistic sensor noise was simulated using signal processing techniques. An LQR controller was designed using state-space methods and tuned through iterative simulation. Root Locus analysis was used to study system behavior, and a high-torque DC motor model was integrated to represent the actuation system. Multiple test scenarios were run to assess controller performance under vibration disturbances.

Key Results

  • The LQR controller successfully reduced drill bit oscillation from approximately ±15° to near 0° within 35 seconds of simulation time.
  • The system demonstrated strong stability and effective disturbance rejection under modeled downhole vibration conditions.
  • Simulation results confirmed fast response and good positioning accuracy without excessive control effort.
  • The project showed that MATLAB/Simulink offers a practical, low-cost platform for testing and validating drilling control strategies before field implementation.
